This article gives a beginner-friendly overview of accessibility and why it matters for real users. It explains how people with different abilities interact with websites using tools like screen readers and keyboard navigation. The article also highlights common accessibility barriers, such as poor color contrast and missing labels, and how they can affect usability. Reading this resource helped me understand that accessibility improves the experience for everyone, not just users with disabilities. Overall, it reinforced the importance of designing websites that are inclusive, usable, and thoughtfully structured.
