Overview

Ethical web development includes respecting user privacy, avoiding misleading design patterns, and building accessible experiences. Legal issues often include copyright, licensing, and compliance requirements.

YouTube Video

This video leads to a series that discusses ethical considerations in web development, including how developers should make decisions that respect user privacy, fairness, and transparency. It explains why ethics matter when writing code that affects real users and how ethical lapses can harm people and erode trust. The video helped me understand that good web development isn't just about functionality, but also about responsibility and user well-being.

Article

Ethical Practices in Web Development: A Guide

This article explains important ethical practices in web development and why developers should think about how their work affects users. It talks about respecting people's privacy, keeping their information safe, and making websites easy to use for everyone, including people with disabilities. The article also explains how making ethical choices helps build trust with users and keeps websites more secure while following the law. Reading this helped me understand that being ethical is not optional for developers, but something they are expected to do. Overall, this resource showed me that good web design should be fair, honest, and focused on respecting users.

Key ideas I will follow

  • Use properly licensed images/fonts and give attribution when required.
  • Be transparent about what a form collects and avoid unnecessary personal data.
  • Avoid deceptive UI (fake buttons, hidden fees, confusing opt-outs).
  • Accessibility is an ethical responsibility, not just a checkbox.
